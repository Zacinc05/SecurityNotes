Distinguishing Scope & Rules of Engagement  
\- Scope is what is to be done  
\- Rules of Engagement is the how  
\- Important to understand that what and how are closely interrelated.  
\- Rescoping may change as you go

Must share communication/contact information  
\- Name, Title, Emergency contact, Secure bulk data transfer method, Agree to incident report process, Share encryption keys  
\- Incase something happens during pen testing (service breaks, real attack, you mess smtn up)

Must agree on social engineering pretexts  
\- Most good attack campaigns will use social engineering  
\- Some companies may have an issue with method/usage  
\- Will need to make sure campaign is reasonable with company, know exactly what to send out  
\- May be important to your job to know popular email traffic (disguise as what’s expected)  
\- May choose targeted attacked that are a lot more nuanced, depends of goal  
\- Person with authority must be in the know on campaign

Test Customer Detection Capability  
\- Giving too much info may compromise attack method  
\- Information gathering, foot printing, scanning/vuln analysis, data aggregation, data exfiltration  
\- Data exfiltration is the scariest thing that can happen to a company (break codes like HIPPA)  
\- Timeline/Times/Location for testing (avoid interfering with business)  
\- Evidence handling (Encryption, Points to stop and call for guidance)  
\- Regular status meetings  
\- Dealing with shunning  
\- Permission to test  
\- Legal considerations (wiretap laws, etc.). Companies may not know all the niche laws.
