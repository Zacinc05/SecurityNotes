Reporting Tips / Best Practice

- Workflow/Documentation  
  - Write as you go mentality  
- Screenshots  
  - Primary evidence for technical findings  
  - Must be clear and defensible  
  - Annotation (point out important parts)  
  - Redaction (hide PII/Passwords and anything else agreed upon)  
  - Professionalism (don’t have dumb wallpaper, username, hostname. Keep simple light background, also helps with printing readability)  
- QA Lifecycle (Quality Assurance phase)  
  - Report should never go from pentester directly to the client  
  - Evaluate  
    - Technical Accuracy  
      - Reproduction steps correct  
    - Consistency  
      - Does executive summary match technical findings  
    - Tone  
      - Is the language professional  
- Final Designation  
  - Are draft processes complete

Appendices

- Deep dive information that would clutter main report unless moved  
- Components  
  - Tool List  
    - Transparency, software used (Nmap, Metasploit, etc.)  
  - Methodology  
    - Explaining the standard followed (i.e. PTES Pen Test Execution Standard)  
  - OSINT & Phishing  
    - Detailed logs of what was found in public records or social eng. Campaign  
  - Literally anything else that requires mentioning
